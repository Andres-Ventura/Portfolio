Andres Ventura
Ex-CEO, Founder

I write code because I help make the world a better place. How do I make the world a better place? While spending hours in deep thought on the matter was effective, I found experiencing things to be the ultimate solution. I took on many titles so early on in life. I was a real estate agent, a startup founder, a CEO, a custodian, a delivery driver, a furniture builder, a retail clerk, an assistant, and the list goes on. But, I never truly felt happy doing all those things. I never felt I was helping anybody and mainly felt like my contributions were nothing but a drop in the oceans of our society. If it weren't for my deep thought sessions; I would have never decided to be a Software Engineer. So here are some takeaways from those sessions, just to name a few.

"Do only what you can speak of with Honour.", "Every Napoleon meets his Watergate.", "The more you know the less you fear.", "Learn how to learn.", "Ask.", "The universe is knowable.", "If you have a why you'll always know your how.", "No one is crazy."

These live in my mind rent-free and I wouldn't charge them for anything in the world. They have helped me find my passion for coding and making solutions to problems that transcend any place and time on earth. It's genuinely remarkable when one grasps the sheer number of possibilities you can unlock with coding.
